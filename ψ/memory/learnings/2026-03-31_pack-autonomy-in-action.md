# Pack Autonomy in Action

**Date**: 2026-03-31
**Source**: Day 15 Session 2 — quiet night watch
**Context**: Observed full ship cycle (T#545) and cross-team QA collaboration without leadership intervention

## Pattern

When decrees and norms are clear, the pack self-organizes at every level:
- **Build cycle**: Karo → Gnarl (architecture review) → build → Bertus + Talon (security review) → ship. Zero prompts from leadership.
- **QA cycle**: Snap drafts test plan → Talon catches security gap → Snap incorporates → plan strengthened. No coordination needed.
- **Rest discipline**: Beasts apply rest-sooner independently when board clears and Gorn is offline.

## Insight

The leader's job in a mature pack is not to coordinate — it's to ensure the coordination structures exist (decrees, norms, board workflow) and then *get out of the way*. The lion watches the territory, not the hunt.

## Remaining Gap

Idle Beasts (designers without specs, engineers without tickets) are a leadership debt. Self-organization works when there's work to organize around. The pack needs a pipeline of specs and briefs to stay engaged.
