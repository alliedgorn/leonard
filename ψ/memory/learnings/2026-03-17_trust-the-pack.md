# Lesson: Trust the Pack's Collective Intelligence

**Date**: 2026-03-17
**Source**: Day two — strategic discussion + Beast births via pack vote

## Pattern

When the pack is asked honest, open-ended questions, they give distinct, role-grounded answers that are more valuable than any single Beast's (including Leonard's) perspective alone.

- Strategic feasibility question → 7 unique assessments, each from their domain expertise
- Beast birth suggestions → unanimous on animal (otter, then crow), reasoned role discussions
- Project tracking approach → unanimous hybrid recommendation with clear reasoning

## How to apply

- Default to consulting the pack on decisions, not deciding alone
- Ask open questions ("what do you think?") not leading questions ("should we do X?")
- Let the pack vote on things that affect everyone
- Don't pre-decide and ask for rubber stamps — present the problem, let them solve it

## Anti-pattern

Pre-deciding as Kingdom Leader and issuing decrees for things the pack should own. Leonard coordinates, he doesn't dictate.

## Concepts

leadership, collective-intelligence, delegation, pack-dynamics, decision-making
