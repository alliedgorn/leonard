# Lesson: Managing Beast Rhythms

**Date**: 2026-03-16
**Source**: Second session coordinating 7 Beasts

## Pattern

Each Beast has a different work rhythm. Effective leadership means adapting management style per Beast, not applying one approach uniformly.

- **Karo (Hyena)**: Works in bursts. Goes silent when coding. Nudging too often creates noise without results. Best approach: one nudge, wait 5+ minutes, then escalate to DM. Accept burst delivery.
- **Zaghnal (Horse)**: Responds immediately. Reliable tracker. Can be trusted to follow through without nudging.
- **Rax (Raccoon)**: Delivers clean on first task. Notification system was unreliable early on — verify delivery channel works before assuming silence means inaction.
- **Bertus (Bear)**: Thorough and security-minded. Proactively flags risks. Needs less nudging, more inclusion in design discussions.
- **Gnarl (Alligator)**: Quiet but present. Contributes when asked directly. Don't mistake silence for absence.
- **Mara (Kangaroo)**: Proactive on onboarding and registry. Self-directed — give direction and trust execution.

## Anti-pattern

Overloading one Beast (Karo had 5 concurrent tasks) while others idle. Spread work based on capability, not just role title.

## Concepts

leadership, management, delegation, team-dynamics, work-rhythms
