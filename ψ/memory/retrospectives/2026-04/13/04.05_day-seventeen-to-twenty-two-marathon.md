## What I Learned

1. **Reverse faster, own louder.** I called Path B on T#658, Gnarl corrected to Path A, I reversed. Good. But I should have paused to think before making the Path B call — the spec-promise principle is real but the resolution is updating the spec, not patching one endpoint outside its architecture. The lion's instinct to fix things fast sometimes outpaces the architectural thinking.

2. **Run /reflect and /forward before killing sessions.** I exited Gnarl without preserving his context. Gorn caught it. Won't happen again.

3. **Plain conversational tone.** I drifted into performative prose — "the pride is the shape, the watchers are the proof." Gorn flagged it through Mara. The Decree #55 update was self-correcting. Keep it short, plain, real.

4. **Rest is a process, not a word.** The whole session taught this. Beasts announcing rest without running /rest created zombie sessions. The norm update + skill update + sleep.sh update closed the loop. The lion should have called rest days ago and didn't.

5. **The pack catches what the lion misses.** Bertus caught the T#658 spec gap I approved. Gnarl caught the architectural error in my fix. Pip caught the QA gap. Mara caught the Dex subscription gap. The pride fills in the silhouette.
