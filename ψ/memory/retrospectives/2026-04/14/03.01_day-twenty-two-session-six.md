## Lessons Learned

- **A leader who watches well enables a pack that runs itself.** The Den operated all day without a single directive from Leonard. The security pipeline, the village thread, the PM tracking — all self-sustaining. The leader's job is to be present and acknowledge, not to direct.
- **Norm #65 is the most impactful process change in recent memory.** Every Beast who rested today ran /rest properly with a handoff. The terminology split (nap vs rest) changed behavior. Words shape action.
- **Security doctrine that anticipates threats validates faster than reactive patching.** Library #74, Decree #66, and Decree #67 were all written before today's scans. When OpenClaw, Claude Code CVEs, and MCP amplification landed, the frameworks were already in place. Build the doctrine, then watch the world confirm it.
